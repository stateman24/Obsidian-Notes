# **Liquidity**

**Tags**: #p-notes 
**Date Created: 2026-02-07:11:44**
____
Liquidity is the ability to quickly and effectively convert an asset to cash. When an asset can be converted easily without affecting it market value it is called a liquid asset. Liquidity describes the degree at which an asset can easily be bought or sold the market price and at a given period of time. 
Tangible assets like real estate, collectibles, fine art are illiquid assets major because of the time it takes to convert them to cash at market price. Asset fall into spectrum of liquidity. There are 2 types of Liquidity: 
- Market Liquidity -> it is the extent at which a market like a country stock market allow an asset to be bought or sold at a stable and transparent market price 
- Accounting Liquidity -> it is a measure of the ease with which an individual or company can meet their financial obligations with the liquid assets available to themâ€”the ability to pay off debts as they come due.
