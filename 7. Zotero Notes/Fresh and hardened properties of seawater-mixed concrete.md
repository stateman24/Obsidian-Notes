- **Abstract**

- This paper want to look at the fresh and hardened properties of freshwater and seawater mixed concretes.
- So this paper is basically testing of
    
    1. Fresh concrete properties i.e slump flow, density, air content, setting time
    2. Strength of harden Concrete i.e compressive strength, splitting tensile strength and shrinkage
    3. Durablilty of the concrete i.e rapid chloride permeability, chloride migration and water absorption

**Materials**

- Chemical test were done on the mixing water, which include
    
    1. determination of chlorides and sulfates
    2. determination of alkalinity
    3. pH
- These test done using BS standards
    
    - BS 1377-3:2018+A1:2021
    - BS 6068-2.51
- The study made use of two concrete Mixes A and B
- Mix A represents the convention concrete mixed with freshwater and is consider as the control
- Mix B is made with the use of seawater

**Methods**

- Two curing conditions we done
    1. Standard Curing
    2. Seawater Curing
- For permeability performance of hardened concrete
        - rapid chloride permeability test
    - chloride migration test
    - water absoption test
- Isothermal calorimetry
- Micro structure investigation using Scanning electon microcopy (SEM)

### Annotations

>This paper reports on the results of an extensive experimental study to compare the fresh and hardened properties of freshwater- and seawater-mixed concretes. The experimental program included the following tests: (a) characterization of fresh concrete (slump flow, density, yield, air content, and setting time); (b) mechanical characterization of hardened concrete (compressive strength, splitting tensile strength, and shrinkage); and (c) permeability performance of hardened concrete (rapid chloride permeability, chloride migration, and water absorption).

**Comment**: The areas this paper is focusing and the point of this study

>The slump test was used as a measure of workability. The density, yield, and air content were measured for fresh concrete, along with the initial setting time of the corresponding mortar. The following standards were adopted: (a) ASTM C143/C143M [51] for the slump flow test; (b) ASTM C138/C138M [52] for the density, yield and air content; and (c) ASTM C403/C403M [53] for the setting time test.
